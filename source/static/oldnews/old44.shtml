Texture Packs - Brian
I mentioned a couple of days ago that we were looking for some people to convert the free textures over to .mat format and send them to us. A ton of people have replied, but Ping_Me has been kind enough to finish them up (ultra-fast, I may add) and upload them to the server. You can download the complete texture pack (containing .mat files) here: http://www.massassi.net/~ping_me/. It weighs in at 3.2Megs, but there are a ton of quality textures, so I recommend this download to all level authors who are sick of the JK texture sets!

While you're at it, download Ping_Me's template re-organization files. These arrange all the 3do's in Jed so it's easier to find what you're looking for. This file can also be found at the above link.

Etc. - Brian
Kiramin, a good friend, sporadic staff member here, and creator of Wayrest Landing (a level released in our level pack), has moved down here from his former home in Oregon. He should be showing his face around here a lot more often (probably more so on Watto's and our other network sites).

So what's up with the site? As usual, a LOT. Slug and the rest of the staff are still deciding exactly what we want to do for the upcoming level contest. I am toiling away at our new news script. It will be used across the network when it's finally done (right now it's almost there, it's in the testing/polishing phase right now). What does this mean to you? Probably not much, but we will be freed from the burden of manually archiving the news, which leaves more time for other stuff.

We're also working on our much delayed Massassi Player's Group - the design is in it's final stages (as a few of you probably saw when we jacked the news page over recently), and the scripts are almost done. Thanks to Dragonfly (of JK Depot) for the initial design. He will be running the site once it's open. I'm looking for a launch next weekend.

After that comes our ULTRA-delayed review section. It has actually been talked about since we started posting levels, but it should soon become a reality. I'm programming a "generic" review script that will interface with our levels and news scripts. We are going to launch it on Shadar Logoth before Massassi, though. This is because I have to re-program our levels section from scratch, as well as make a converter to convert the old format to the new. The new will sort the levels by alphabetical order, as well as allow you to search by author or sort by date. I really hope to roll this out on Massassi within a month, but with so much stuff going on offline, I don't know if I can meet that deadline. I will do my best.

Finally, I would like to give a huge thanks to the staff that have been keeping the site up. Also a big thanks to blujay for keeping up on the Screenshot of the Day, as well as his work to program a script for it.
